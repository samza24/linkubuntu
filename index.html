The problem

Every day in South Africa, people die or are critically injured in road accidents. Their families wait hours, days — sometimes years — to find out what happened. Bodies are buried as paupers because no one could identify them in time.
The current system fails because it relies on a single phone number on an ID that may be outdated. When that number is dead, the family connection is broken.

How LinkUbuntu works

1

Citizens build their contact web

Download the app. Link your spouse, parents, siblings, friends — everyone who needs to know if something happens to you. They confirm the link. The web stays updated because people update it themselves.

2

Responder scans at the scene

A SAPS officer, paramedic, or traffic officer uses the responder app to scan the fingerprint of an accident victim. The system matches against the Home Affairs database and returns the person's ID.

3

Family is notified instantly

LinkUbuntu finds the person's contact web and sends SMS and push notifications to all confirmed contacts in priority order. Family knows within minutes — not days.
